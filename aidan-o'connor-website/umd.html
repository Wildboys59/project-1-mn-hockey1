Minnesota Duluth Bulldogs

The Minnesota Duluth Bulldogs Hockey Program was founded in 1930.

The Minnesota Duluth Bulldogs men's hockey program has a rich history, initially competing at the club level, they became a member of the NCAA in 1961. The Bulldogs joined the Western Collegiate Hockey Association (WCHA) in 1971, where they became competitive quickly. Under the leadership of notable coaches, including Mike Sertich and Scott Sandelin, the program rose to prominence. The Bulldogs have won multiple WCHA championships and, in 2011, they captured their first NCAA championship, followed by another title in 2018. The team is known for its passionate fan base and strong rivalries, particularly with the University of Minnesota and St. Cloud State University. The Bulldogs have produced numerous NHL players and are recognized for their commitment to excellence on and off the ice. Their consistent performance has made them a prominent program in college hockey.
