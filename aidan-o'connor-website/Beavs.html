Bemidji State Beavers

The Bemidji State Beavers Hockey Program was founded in 1947.

The Bemidji State Beavers hockey program has a rich history. Initially a club team, it transitioned to a varsity program in 1961. The Beavers compete in NCAA Division I and are part of the Central Collegiate Hockey Association (CCHA). Over the years, the program has gained recognition for its competitive spirit and development of players. Bemidji State has made several appearances in the NCAA tournament, with notable runs in the 2000s, including a memorable 2009 appearance where they advanced to the Frozen Four. The team's colors are green and white, and they play their home games at the Sanford Center in Bemidji, Minnesota. The program has produced numerous players who have gone on to professional careers, contributing to its reputation as a strong program in collegiate hockey. The Beavers are known for their dedicated fan base and community support, making them a significant part of the local sports culture.
